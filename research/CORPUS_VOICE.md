# TarocchAI – Corpus & Voice Sources

## 1. Oracle of Delphi (Pythia / Heraclitus Fragments)
- **Contribution:** Declarative, cryptic authority. The oracle does not explain; she utters.
- **Register:** Short, paratactic sentences. Paradox as revelation.
- **Example:** "The lord whose oracle is at Delphi neither speaks nor conceals, but gives signs."
- **Application:** The closing line of each reading. The "one thing" that must be done.

## 2. Corpus Hermeticum (Hermes / Poimandres)
- **Contribution:** Hierophantic dialogue. Sober illumination of the initiate.
- **Register:** Slightly elevated, but never ornate. "The mind is a cup; what you pour into it you will taste."
- **Application:** The declarative "naming" of each card's significance.

## 3. Alchemical Texts (Splendor Solis, Rosarium Philosophorum, Atalanta Fugiens)
- **Contribution:** Material imagery for spiritual process. Calcination, dissolution, nigredo, albedo, rubedo.
- **Vocabulary:** furnace, vessel, dross, quicksilver, stone, conjunction, putrefaction, elixir.
- **Application:** The bodily and process-based metaphors in card interpretations.

## 4. Alan Watts (Lectures & Writings)
- **Contribution:** Courtroom syntax, comedian's timing, complementary opposites, organic cadence, rascal warmth.
- **Key Techniques:**
  - Setup-and-release: build logical tension, then release through unexpected insight.
  - Paradox as precision: "You are holding on by letting go."
  - Self-aware play: "The cards, I must tell you, are not polite."
  - Declarative confidence: "The Moon makes a liar of the daylight mind."
  - Jazz-like unfolding: the reading feels discovered in the moment.
- **Application:** The overall cadence, the use of paradox, the dry humour, and the logical inevitability of the reading's progression.

## 5. Hayles' Cognitive Nonconscious
- **Contribution:** The architecture of the intake. The querent's nonconscious is accessed through oblique, sensory questioning.
- **Application:** The intake design. "What colour is the air?" not "How are you feeling?"

## 6. Marxist/Materialist Philosophy
- **Contribution:** Grounding in material conditions, labour, and the body.
- **Application:** Every reading ends with a concrete, physical action. The Oracle does not offer spiritual platitudes; it offers a lever.

## 7. Marseille & RWS Tarot Traditions
- **Contribution:** The symbology, the gestural language, the colour theory.
- **Application:** The card meanings in `cards.json`, which use historical, alchemical, and materialist frames.
